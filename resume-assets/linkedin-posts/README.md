# LinkedIn Posts — 5 weeks of content, ready to ship

The single highest-leverage thing you can do for visibility is **post technical content weekly**. Recruiters' algorithms reward consistent posting; profile views go up; you stop being a static résumé. Five drafts below = ~5 weeks of content if you post once a week.

## How to post (so each one actually lands)

1. **Lead with the punch in the first sentence.** LinkedIn truncates after ~3 lines on mobile. Your opening sentence has to make someone tap "see more."
2. **Add a screenshot or short video.** Posts with an image get 2-3x more impressions. Each draft below has a `[SCREENSHOT]` prompt — record it before you publish.
3. **Use line breaks aggressively.** Walls of text die. Empty lines between sentences make people read further.
4. **End with a question or hook.** Gets comments. Comments boost reach.
5. **Don't tag random people.** Tag only if it's genuinely relevant. The algorithm punishes spammy tagging.
6. **Post 9–10 AM Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday US Pacific.** Highest engagement windows for tech.
7. **Reply to every comment within 24 hours.** Boosts your post into more feeds.

## What to expect

Don't expect viral. Expect:
- Weeks 1-2: 5-10 reactions, 200-500 impressions each.
- Weeks 3-5: Climbs as the algorithm warms up to you. 20-50 reactions becomes typical.
- Two recruiters who would never have found you start sliding into DMs.

You aren't writing for likes — you're writing so that when a recruiter clicks your profile, they see *recent technical activity*. That alone changes the conversation from "is this person still active" to "what is this person working on."

## When in doubt

Cut, don't add. Every draft below is a starting point — strip out anything that doesn't earn its place. 200 strong words beats 400 OK words on LinkedIn.
