# Cover Letter Templates

Three drafts tuned to different role types. **Read once, then customize per company** — the opening paragraph and the "why you" paragraph should always cite something specific about the company. The bones stay the same.

## When to use which

| File | Use when | Anchor |
|---|---|---|
| `01-generic-senior-ic.md` | Generic full-stack IC role at a product company | TableCaptain V2 #1-by-adoption result |
| `02-ai-devtools.md` | Companies hiring for AI integration, dev tools, MCP, agent infra | Holoscene MCP + multi-agent pipeline |
| `03-realtime-infra.md` | Real-time, streaming, multi-screen, or 24/7-uptime products | TableCaptain real-time sync at 30+ venues |

## Universal customization checklist

Before sending any of these:

1. Replace `[COMPANY]` and `[HIRING MANAGER]` (use the actual name — find it on LinkedIn).
2. Replace `[SPECIFIC THING ABOUT THE COMPANY]` with something you actually noticed. *"Your post about scaling your WebSocket layer last month"*, *"Your decision to use Electron for the desktop client"*, *"Your hiring announcement said you're moving from REST to tRPC"*. **Generic flattery gets thrown out.** Specificity gets read.
3. Tighten to ~250 words. Hiring managers spend 20 seconds on this. Make every sentence earn its place.
4. End with a clear ask: "I'd love a 20-minute call this week" beats "Looking forward to hearing from you."

## Universal "do not" list

- Don't write a wall of text about your career arc. They have your résumé.
- Don't open with "I am writing to express my interest in..." — every recruiter has seen 10,000 of those today.
- Don't list skills. The résumé does that. The cover letter is for *signal* — why this company, what you bring that's not on the page.
- Don't mention salary, location, or relocation in the cover letter. That's a screen conversation.
